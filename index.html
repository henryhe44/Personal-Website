Short Blurb About Me


If the giant letters on the top of this page doesn't give it away (totally a narcissistic tendency), my name is Henry - a Junior of CUNY Queens aiming for a BA in both Computer Science and Economics. I'm an awfully frank guy with his own set of quirks and a fondness for fun!

Right now, I intern at the Queens College Tech Incubator to provide startups the opportunity to grow into successful tech companies. I also take on the administrative duties of a secretary for the Queens College Association for Computing Machinery, which includes menial paperwork, policy writing, and notify members of career opportunities. More recently, I helped organize several Arduino Workshops to teach the basics of electronics and build projects involving the microcontroller.

In my free time, I volunteer for the Tzu Chi Foundation and had the pleasure of working with its energetic members! Street sweeping, coin counting and mail packaging would be mundane tasks if it weren't for the lively men and women that make up the organization. I was honored to be a part of the Tzu Chi Annual Health Day, where I helped provide medical service to over 200 people!

I've done lots of exploring ever since my interest for technology was sparked by my participation in the 2013 NYGentech program, but I'm still not entirely sure where I want to be. Do I want to use my economic expertise and go into fintech? Do I want to use my OOP proficiency to become a software engineer? Or maybe I should continue the path of an aspiring full-stack developer? I'm fine with anything really, as long as I'm part of the inviting and dynamic culture that technology has built!
